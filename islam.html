Islam

Overview The Five Pillars Of Islam Six Main Beliefs Holidays Fun Facts

Islam is an Abrahamic, monotheistic religion teaching that there is only one God, and that Muhammad is the messenger of God. In Islam, God(Allah) is the all-powerful and all-knowing ruler of the universe, and the creator of everything in existence. Muslims believe that Allah is shapeless and does not resemble his creations.

The Five Pillars of Islam:

  1. Shahadah: sincerely reciting the Muslim profession of faith.
  2. Salat: performing ritual prayers in the proper way five times each day.
  3. Zakat: paying an alms (or charity) tax to benefit the poor and the needy.
  4. Sawm: fasting during the month of Ramadan.
  5. Hajj:pilgrimage to Mecca.


Six Main Beliefs:

  1. Belief in Allah as the one and only God.
  2. Belief in angels.
  3. Belief in the holy books.
  4. Belief in the Prophets...e.g. Adam, Ibrahim (Abraham), Musa (Moses), Dawud (David), Isa (Jesus). Muhammad is the final prophet.
  5. Belief in the Day of Judgement.
  6. Belief in Predestination. Muslims believe that Allah has the knowlege of all that will happen, but they also believe that human beings have freedom of choice.

Islamic Holidays:

    Al-Hijra is the Islamic New Year, which marks the end of Mohammad's journey from Mecca to Medina.
    Ramadan is the Muslim month of fasting.
    Prophet's Birthday is the Celebration of Prophet Muhammad's Birth.
    Eid is celebrated twice a year.
    1) Eid ul-Adha is the festival of sacrifice.
    2) Eid ul-Fitr celebrates the end of Ramadan.

Prayer Essentials:

Muslims pray 5 times a day at set times. Prayer occurs at dawn(fajr), noon(dhuhr), afternoon(asr), evening(maghrib), and night(isha).
Prayer includes a series of movements, supplications, and recitations from the Quran.
It is mandatory for each Muslim to pray by the time they reach the age of puberty.
A Muslim may pray anywhere, including school, work, home, or fields.
They use prayer rugs to keep their prayer area clean.
