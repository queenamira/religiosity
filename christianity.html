Christianity
Overview Holy Text Holidays Christianity Today Catholicism Orthodox Protestantism

Christianity is an Abrahamic monotheistic religion that stems from the life, teachings, death, and resurrection of Jesus of Nazareth, otherwise known as Christ. Christians believe that Jesus was the son of God and by following his divine example they can have a heavenly life both while they are living and while they are dead.

Holy Text

The Holy Bible is the sacred text for Christians. The Holy Bible is broken up into two parts: the Old Testament, which consists of the stories found in the Jewish Bible, and the New Testament, which consists of the stories of Jesus' life and teachings.
In addition to the story of Jesus and his teachings, there are other texts important to Christians such as the letters to Paul and the Book of Revelations.
Different sects of Christianity may have more sacred texts in additon to the Holy Bible.

Holidays

The biggest holidays for Christians are Christmas and Easter. Christmas celebrates the birth of Jesus, and Easter is the day that they believe Jesus was resurrected after dying on the cross.




Christianity Today

There are three main sects of Christianity: Catholic, Orthodox, and Protestant.

Catholicism

This is different than the other branches in that Catholics follow the Pope who is the head of the Catholic church. Catholics believe in the Eucharist and transubstantiation which is when Catholics eat blessed wine and bread that they believe has been transformed into the body and blood of Jesus. This tradition comes from the Last Supper when Jesus said that the wine and bread on the table was his body.

Orthodox

This is another of the main branches of Christianity. The word orthodox comes from the Greek word meaning "right belief." Orthodox Christianity is primarily practiced in areas that were once part of the Eastern Roman Emipire. Orthodox Christianity shares many of same beliefs as the other branches, but it differs in how they worship and what traditions are practiced. Orthodox Christianity stresses the importance of worship and prayer as an essential part of life. Until 1918, Orthodox Christianity followed the Julian calender for dates of holidays. The calendar is thirteen days behind the more commonly used Gregorian calender. Since 1918, most Orthodox Christians adopted the Gregorian calender for most holidays.


Protestantism

This is one of the main sects of Christianity. Protestantism was started in 1517 when Martin Luther nailed 95 theses (or issues with the Catholic church) to a castle in Wittenburg. Protestant sects differ from Catholicism in that they do not follow the Pope. Some of the major Protestant sects in the United States are Baptist, Methodist, and Pentecostal. One of the main beliefs of Baptist Protestantism is that baptism, a tradition for babies to clease them of the first sin (Eve eating the fruit of the Tree of Knowledge). It is believed that without baptism you can not go to heaven. Baptists believe once one can fully profess their belief and devotion to God then they can get baptized. Methodism is another sect of Protestantism. Its main principles are: all need to be saved; all may be saved; all may know themselves saved; all may be saved to the uttermost. Pentecostal Protestantism is another of the major sects in the United States. They believe that the Bible and faith in Jesus can transform one's life.

Today, Christianity is the most practiced religion in the world with two billion followers.
