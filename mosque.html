Mosque(Islam)

Hover over the image to read about Mosques.

A mosque is a place of worship for Muslims. The Arabic word comes from the root “sa-ja-da” meaning to prostrate. When a Muslim’s forehead touches the ground, he or she is close to God.

Muslims must be clean before they pray. They make sure of this by performing ritual washing, called wudhu. Mosques have washing facilities.

Praying together in a congregation helps Muslims to realise that all humanity is one, and all are equal in the sight of Allah.

Muslims can pray anywhere, but it is especially good to pray with others in a mosque. Prayer establishes the connection between the believer and his Lord and prostration symbolises complete submission. There is no need of a priest as an intermediary. (While there is a prayer leader in the mosque - the imam - they are not a priest, simply a person who knows a great deal about Islam.)
